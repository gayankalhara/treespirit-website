“The equivalent of 36 football fields of forest are clear-cut every day.”

- World Wildlife Fund

Illegal logging has been identified as a major problem in the world, with studies indicating that more than 100 million cubic metres of timber are still being cut illegally each year. Furthermore, destruction of the world’s forests contributes up to 20% of global carbon dioxide gas emissions.

As a direct result of illegal logging, governments in the world lose billions of dollars in timber sales revenue, fostering corruption and undermining the rule of law. Further, Illegal logging contributes to a wide range of environmental issues such as loss of biodiversity, erosion, water pollution, landslides, and forest fires

About TreeSpirit

TreeSpirit is a Forest Monitoring System based on Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) and Chainsaw Noise Identification using a Convolutional Neural Network.
