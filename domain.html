Literature Survey

Various attempts have been made to carry out effective surveillance of large forestcovered areas such as,

  • Satellite image processing
  • Local video surveillance
  • Detection of acoustic signals of chainsaws
  • Detection of vibrations resultant of tree felling

Currently, the optimal and the only feasible surveillance strategy against illegal logging is the detection of acoustic signals of chainsaw considering the practicalities of the logging industry. This position is further strengthened by the fact that many available studies as well as existing commercial implementations focus on identification of illegal logging through detection of acoustic signals of chainsaws.

Detection of acoustic signals of a chainsaw is the only feasible method to monitor forests for illegal logging activities at present. Hence, the focus in this section will be on analysing the existing research which focuses on forest monitoring systems through Sound recognition techniques under three criteria, which are,

  1. Architecture and Communication Techniques
  2. Sound Recognition Techniques
  3. Sound Localization Techniques

Research Gap

Several studies have been carried out regarding various theoretical aspects of WSN based forest monitoring systems, yet it has not yet been fully demonstrated how these multiple studies can be incorporated to form one efficient, scalable and practical forest monitoring architecture which has the potential to be commercialized. Several shortcomings of existing studies and products were identified such as,

  1. Forest Environment Contamination due to accumulation of e-waste due to airdropping of sensors.
  2. Peer to Peer Communication Technologies can result in communication breakdown in case the nodes in the middle of the network get damaged.
  3. Utilization of GSM signals for communication limits the scalability of the monitoring network.
  4. Lack of Sound Localization techniques decreases the accuracy of a possible detection.

Therefore, the purpose of this research will be to propose a cost-effective practical implementation of a WSN based forest monitoring architecture which overcome the above-mentioned shortcomings.

Research Problem

Many governments and international organizations have taken many steps to curb illegal logging activities but with little success. One of the reasons for this is that it is difficult to monitor large tropical forests for illegal logging as government entities and forest conservation organizations often lack the manpower needed to effectively patrol all threatened areas. Because of this, illegal logging is being carried out at an everincreasing rate with relative impunity. As a solution to this various WSN based forest monitoring techniques have been proposed, but a general concrete practical solution not constrained by different factors does not exist yet.

Methodology

The solution consists of,
  1. Listening Posts
  2. Sound Processors
  3. Base Station
  4. Cloud Server & SMS Gateway
  5. Web Application

Listening Posts

The Listening Post is the most elementary component of the system. It is a system that consists of a Digital MEMS Microphone, A Real Time Clock (RTC), A high speed – low range communication chip and a Temperature + Humidity Sensor. Its main purpose is to transmit live sounds from its surroundings to the allocated Sound Processor for 20 seconds every minute along with the Temperature and Humidity data it has collected at the moment. In fact, it is correct to identify it as a Sensor node which is continuously transmitting data which can be then interpreted by the higher components of the system.

Sound Processor

The Sound Processor contains Software modules to analyse the sounds to identify chainsaw sounds and if such sounds are identified, estimate the origin of the such sounds. These two modules will be identified as “Sound Recognition Module” and “Sound Localization Module” here on respectively. It also has hardware modules to communicate with the Listening posts assigned to it as well as the Base Station.

Upon the receival of a sound, the Sound Processor reacts in two ways depending on the outcome of the analysis in the Sound Recognition Module.

  • Case 1 – Sound is identified as a chainsaw sound
  • Case 2 – Sound cannot be identified as a chainsaw sound

In the case 1, once the received signal is identified as having a strong probability to be a chainsaw sound, the Sound Processor then activates the Sound Localization module it contains. The sound localization will be carried out using a method called Multilateration. Once the localization is complete, the Sound Processor will then transmit an alert indicating possible logging operation at the given coordinates/ approximate area in the forest over an ISM Band Radio Link to the Base Station. This operation happens real-time.


In the case 2, the Sound Processor will ignore the Sound and avoid taking further actions.

Base Station

Once an alert is reached to the base station via a Sound Processor, base station uploads the data to a cloud server.

Cloud Server & SMS Gateway

The server publishes the data through a REST API which will be used to drive the, Web Application and SMS Gateway which sends real-time alerts to predefined entities.

Web Application

The Web Application aggregates and displays all the locations and alerts transmitted by a base station device in an interactive map. Further, it provides features to view and analyze historical data as well as information about Clusters.
